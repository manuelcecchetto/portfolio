MDX becomes most useful when writing teams can stay in narrative flow while still
dropping in reusable visual components with predictable styling.

<Callout title="When to reach for MDX" tone="cool">
  Use MDX for long-form posts that need explanatory components. Keep simpler updates
  as plain Markdown to preserve writing speed.
</Callout>

Instead of hard-coding a one-off image style in every article, a shared figure
component keeps rhythm and caption treatment consistent.

<ImageFigure
  src="/images/blog/mdx-authoring-workflow.svg"

/>

The same approach also works for short implementation references.

<CodeNote
  title="Collection loader pattern"
  language="ts"
  code={`const blog = defineCollection({
  loader: glob({
    base: "./src/data/blog",
    pattern: "**/*.{md,mdx}",
  }),
  schema: blogEntrySchema,
});`}
/>

With this setup, posts stay expressive without fragmenting the visual language of
the site.
